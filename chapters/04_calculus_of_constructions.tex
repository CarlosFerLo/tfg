\chapter{Calculus of Constructions}

\section{Syntactic Formulation}

In this section, we will formally define the syntax of Calculus of Constructions.
We will use the approach of Thomas Streicher in \cite{streicher} as the original 
definition in \cite{coquand1988} is characterized by its syntax overloading, 
making it much harder to follow.
They just use $[x: A]B$ to present product types, functional abstraction and 
universal quantification. Also, properties are presented as terms of type $Prop$
and as types themselves. To solve the latter, we will be using a type constructor
$Proof$ that given a proposition $p : Prop$ it will give $Proof(p)$ the type of 
proofs of proposition $p$.

First, we will define the class of \textbf{pre-well-formed type expressions},
ranged over by capital letters, 
and the class of \textbf{pre-well-formed object expressions},
ranged over by lower case letters, by mutual recursion in a BMF-like way: % TODO: add definition of BMF 
\[
\begin{array}{lll}
  E \Coloneqq & (\Pi x : E)\, E & \text{product of types} \\
        & \mid\ \Prop & \text{type of propositions} \\
        & \mid\ \Proof(t) & \text{type of proofs of proposition } t
\end{array}
\]
\[
\begin{array}{lll}
    t \Coloneqq & x & \text{variable} \\
        & \mid\ (\lambda x \colon E) t & \text{functional abstraction} \\
        & \mid\ \App([x \colon E ] E, t, t) & \text{function application} \\
        & \mid\ (\forall x \colon E) t & \text{universal quantification} 

\end{array}
\]
We say a \textbf{pre-context} is a syntactical expression of the form 
$x_1 : A_1, \dots, x_n : A_n$, where $x_1, \dots, x_n$ are pairwise 
syntactically different variables. 
Contexts are ranged over by capital greek letters.
We define $\Var(\Gamma)$ as the set of variables declared in $\Gamma$.
We will not distinguish expressions which are equivalent up to renaming of variables
($\alpha$-conversion), to do this we will be using \textit{deBruijn indices}
\footnote{
    \textit{deBruijn indices} are a method to refer to declarations not by name (variables)
    but by numbers which tell how far one has to go back into the current context of an expression
    to find the declaration of the thing it refers to.   
}.
We define a \textbf{pre-judgment} to be a syntactic expression of one of the following forms:
\[
\begin{array}{ll}
    A \type & A \text{is a type} \\
    A = B & A \text{ and } B \text{ are equal types} \\
    t : A & t \text{ is an object of type } A \\
    t = s : A & t \text{ and } s \text{ are equal objects of type } $A$
\end{array}
\]
we will use the letter $\mathcal{J}$ to refer to pre-judgments.
We define a \textbf{pre-sequent} as a syntactic expression of the following form
\[
\begin{array}{ll}
    \Gamma \ok & \Gamma \text{ is a context} \\
    \Gamma = \Delta & \Gamma \text{ and } \Delta \text{ are equal contexts} \\
    \Gamma \Rightarrow \mathcal{J} & \text{the judgement } \mathcal{J} \text{ holds w.r.t. } \Gamma
\end{array}
\]


Finally we define the set of \textbf{sequents}, i.e. valid pre-sequents, 
to be generated by the following set of rules. 
Sequents are ranged over by capital italic letters.
This set of rules is more extent that the original in order to make explicit rules for 
handling contexts and to add more rules stating equality of types and objects.

When presenting the axiomatization of Calculus of Constructions we shall use the following notation:
\[
    \ddoublefrac{S_1}{S_2} \text{ abbreviates } \dfrac{S_1}{S_2} \text{ and } \dfrac{S_2}{S_1}
\]

Context Formation Rules:
\begin{enumerate} % TODO: reformat this
    \item[\textsc{Empty}] $\dfrac{}{\quad \ok}$
    \item[\textsc{Cont-Intro}] $\ddoublefrac{\Gamma \Rightarrow A \type}{\Gamma, x \colon A \ok}$, if $x \notin \Var(\Gamma)$
    \item[\textsc{Cont-Thin}] $\dfrac{
            \Gamma, \Delta \ok \quad \Gamma \Rightarrow A \type
        }{
            \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta \ok
        }$, if $x \notin \Var(\Gamma) \cup \Var(\Delta)$
    \item[\textsc{Cont-Sub}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta \ok \quad \Gamma \Rightarrow t \colon A
    }{
        \Gamma, \Delta[x \mid\ t] \ok
    }$
\end{enumerate}

Context Equality Rules
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[\textsc{Crefl}] $\ddoublefrac{
        \Gamma = \Gamma
    }{
        \Gamma \ok
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Csym}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma = \Delta
    }{
        \Delta = \Gamma
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Ctrans}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma = \Delta \quad \Delta = \Theta
    }{
        \Gamma = \Theta
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Cequ}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta \ok 
        \quad 
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A = B
    }{
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta
        =
        \Gamma, x : B, \Delta
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Creflect1}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A 
        = 
        \Delta, x \colon B
    }{
        \Gamma = \Delta
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Creflect2}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A 
        = \Delta, x \colon B
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A = B_1
    }$
\end{enumerate}

Structural Rules for Judgement Formation
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[\textsc{Ass}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta \ok
    }{
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta 
        \Rightarrow 
        x \colon A
    }$ 
    \item[\textsc{Reflection1}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow \mathcal{J}
    }{
        \Gamma \ok
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Reflection2}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t : A
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A \type
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Thin}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, \Delta \Rightarrow \mathcal{J}
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A \type   
    }{
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta 
        \Rightarrow 
        \mathcal{J}
    }$, if $x \notin \Var(\Gamma) \cup \Var(\Delta)$
    \item[\textsc{Sub}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta \Rightarrow \mathcal{J}
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t : A  
    }{
        \Gamma, \Delta[x \mid\ t] 
        \Rightarrow
        \mathcal{J}[x \mid\ t]
    }$
\end{enumerate}

Equality Rules:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[\textsc{Equ}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma = \Delta
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow \mathcal{J}
    }{
        \Delta \Rightarrow \mathcal{J}
    }$ 
    \item[\textsc{Refl-Typ}] $\ddoublefrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A = A
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A \type
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Refl-Obj}] $\ddoublefrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t = t : A
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t : A
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Symm-Typ}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A = B
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow B = A
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Symm-Obj}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t = s : A
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow s = t : A
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Trans-Typ}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A = B 
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow B = C
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A = C
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Trans-Obj}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t_1 = t_2 : A 
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t_2 = t_3 : A
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t_1 = t_3 : A
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Repl1}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t = s : A
        \quad
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta \ok
    }{
        \Gamma, \Delta[x \mid\ t] 
        =
        \Gamma, \Delta[x \mid\ t]
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Repl2}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t = s : A
        \quad
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Gamma \Rightarrow B \type
    }{
        \Gamma, \Delta[x \mid\ t]
        \Rightarrow
        B[x \mid\ t] = B[x \mid\ s]
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Repl3}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t_1 = t_2 : A
        \quad
        \Gamma, x \colon A, \Delta \Rightarrow s : B
    }{
        \Gamma, \Delta[ x \mid\ t_1]
        \Rightarrow
        s[x \mid\ t_1] = s[x \mid\ t_2] : B[x \mid\ t_1]
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Conv1}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A = B
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t : A
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t : B
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Conv2}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A = B
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t = s : A
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t = s : B
    }$
\end{enumerate}

Rules for Products of types

\begin{enumerate}
    \item[\textsc{$\prod$-Form}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A \Rightarrow B \type
    }{
        \Gamma 
        \Rightarrow 
        (\prod x \colon A) B \type
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\prod$-Equ}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A_1 = A_2
        \quad
        \Gamma, x \colon A_1 \Rightarrow B_1 = B_2
    }{
        \Gamma
        \Rightarrow
        (\prod x \colon A_1) B_1
        =
        (\prod x \colon A_2) B_2
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\prod$-Intro}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A \Rightarrow t : B
    }{
        \Gamma
        \Rightarrow
        (\lambda x \colon A) t
        :
        (\prod x \colon A) B
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\xi$-Rule}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A_1 \Rightarrow t_1 = t_2 : B
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A_1 = A_2
    }{
        \Gamma
        \Rightarrow
        (\lambda x \colon A_1) t_1
        =
        (\lambda x \colon A_2) t_2
        \colon
        (\prod x \colon A_1) B
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\prod$-Elim}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t \colon (\prod x \colon A) B
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow s \colon A
        \quad
        \Gamma, x \colon A \Rightarrow B \type
    }{
        \Gamma
        \Rightarrow
        \App([x \colon A] B, t, s) 
        \colon 
        B[x \mid\ s]
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\prod$-Elim-Equ}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A_1 = A_2
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t_1 = t_2 \colon (\prod x \colon A_1) B_1
        \quad
        \Gamma, x \colon A_1 \Rightarrow B_1 = B_2
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow s_1 = s_2 \colon A_1
    }{
        \Gamma
        \Rightarrow
        \App([x \colon A_1] B_1, t_1, s_1)
        = 
        \App([x \colon A_2] B_2, t_2, s_2)
        \colon
        B_1[x \mid\ s_1]
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\beta$-Rule}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A \Rightarrow B \type
        \quad
        \Gamma, x \colon A \Rightarrow t \colon B
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow s \colon A
    }{
        \Gamma 
        \Rightarrow
        \App([x \colon A] B, (\lambda x : A) t, s)
        = 
        t[x \mid\ s] 
        \colon
        B[x \mid\ s]
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\eta$-rule}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A \Rightarrow B \type
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow t \colon (\prod x \colon A) B
    }{
        \Gamma 
        \Rightarrow
        (\lambda x \colon A) \App([x \colon A] B, t, x) 
        = 
        t
        \colon
        (\prod x \colon A) B
    }$
\end{enumerate}

Rules for Propositions

\begin{enumerate}
    \item[\textsc{Prop}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma \ok
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow \Prop \type
    }$ 
    \item[\textsc{Prop-Type}] $\ddoublefrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow p \colon \Prop
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow \Proof(p) \type
    }$
    \item[\textsc{Prop-Equ}] $\ddoublefrac{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow p_1 = p_2 \colon \Prop
    }{
        \Gamma \Rightarrow \Proof(p_1) = \Proof(p_2)
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\forall$-Intro}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A 
        \Rightarrow
        p \colon \Prop
    }{
        \Gamma
        \Rightarrow
        (\forall x \colon A) p 
        \colon \Prop
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\forall$-Equ}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A_1 \Rightarrow p_1 = p_2 \colon \Prop
        \quad
        \Gamma \Rightarrow A_1 = A_2
    }{
        \Gamma
        \Rightarrow
        (\forall x \colon A_1) p_1
        =
        (\forall x \colon A_2) p_2 
        \colon
        \Prop
    }$
    \item[\textsc{$\forall$-Elim}] $\dfrac{
        \Gamma, x \colon A \Rightarrow p \in \Prop
    }{
        \Gamma 
        \Rightarrow
        \Proof((\forall x \colon A) p)
        = 
        (\prod x \colon A) \Proof(p)
    }$
\end{enumerate}

We will write $\vdash \mathcal{J}$ to say that $\mathcal{J}$ is a derivable judgement.

\section{Doctrines of Constructions}

We will leverage the concept of context categories introduced in the earlier chapter
to serve as a basic structure for handling dependent types, and add more conditions
so that it can handle products of families of types and the type of properties, in
order to develop a categorical structure that captures the interpretation of the
Calculus of constructions.

First, we will introduce product types into our semantics, but to do so, we need
the notion of section of an object:
\begin{definition}
    Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a contextual category, $A$ and $B$ be objects of $\mathcal{C}$ such that $A \lhd B$,
    the set of \textbf{sections} of $B$ is defined as 
    \begin{equation*}
        \Sections(B) \coloneqq \{ s \colon A \to B  \mid\ p(B) \circ s = \id_A\}
    \end{equation*}
\end{definition}
If $\level(B) = 1$, then 
\[
    \Sections(B) = \{ s \mid\ s \colon 1 \to B \}
\]
as any morphism $s \colon 1 \to B$ satisfies $p(B) \circ s = \id_1$ as 
$1$ is a terminal object. In this case, the notion of section
matches the notion of \textit{global element} of $B$.

\begin{definition}
    A \textbf{contextual category with products of families of types} is a category $\mathcal{C}$
    together with two mappings $\prod$ and $\eval$ defined over the collection of objects $B$ of 
    $\mathcal{C}$ with $level(B) \geq 2$, such that:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item whenever $1 \lhd A \lhd B$, then $1 \lhd \prod(B)$ and $eval_B \colon p(A)^*\prod(B) \to B$
              is a morphism over $A$
              \[
              \begin{tikzcd}
                p(A)^*\prod(B) \arrow[rr, "\eval_B"] \arrow[dr, "p(p(A)^*\prod(B))"', open]&& B \arrow[dl, "p(B)", open] \\
                & A &
              \end{tikzcd}
              \]
              such that for all $s \in \Sections(B)$ there is a unique section $t \in \Sections(\prod (B))$
              with $\eval_B \circ p(A)^*t = s$. This unique section $t$ is called $\curry(s)$.
              \[
                \begin{tikzcd}
                A \arrow[rr, open] \arrow[ddd, "\id_A"', bend right=85] \arrow[d, "p(A)^*t"] \arrow[dd, "s"', bend right=80] &  & 1 \arrow[d, "t"'] \arrow[ddd, "\id_1", bend left=80]   \\
                p(A)^*\prod(B) \arrow[d, "\eval_B"] \arrow[rr, "{q(p(A), \prod(B))}"']                                       &  & \prod(B) \arrow[dd, open]                              \\
                B \arrow[d, open]                                                                                                  &  &                                                  \\
                A \arrow[rr, open]                                                                                                 &  & 1                                               
                \end{tikzcd}
              \]
              \begin{observation}
                as any section $t$ of $\prod(B)$ gives a section $eval_B \circ p(A)^*t$ of $B$, hence we have a
                canonical 1-1-correspondence of $\Sections(B)$ and $\Sections(\prod(B))$. 
              \end{observation}
        \item and for any object $J$ and any morphism $f : J \to 1$ the following conditions are satisfied
              \[
                f^*(\prod(B)) = \prod(f^*B) 
                \quad \text{and} \quad 
                f^*(eval_B) = eval_{f^*B}
              \]
    \end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

It follows from the definition that substitution preserves functional abstraction or currying:
\begin{lemma}
    Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a contextual category with products of families of types.
    Let $1 \lhd A \lhd B$ be objects and $f \colon J \to 1$ a morphism in $\mathcal{C}$.
    If $t$ is a section of $B$, then 
    \[
        f^*(\curry(B)) = \curry(f^*B) 
    \]
\end{lemma}

Notice that in any contextual category with products of families of types, the following equations hold:
\begin{align*}
    \qquad & \mathrm{eval}_B \circ p(A)^*\mathrm{curry}(s) = s \tag{$\beta$} \\[1em]
    \qquad & \mathrm{curry}(\mathrm{eval}_B \circ p(A)^*t) = t \tag{$\eta$}
\end{align*}
The first equation corresponds to the $\beta$-rule of typed $\lambda$-calculi 
and the second equation corresponds to the $\eta$-rule of typed $\lambda$-calculi.
Contextual Categories with products correspond to $\lambda$-calculus with dependent types.

Now we will add the type of propositions. To do it, we shall find objects in our category whose 
sections corresponds, i.e. its terms, correspond to objects.
\begin{definition}
    Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a context category, an \textbf{enumeration of types of} $\mathcal{C}$
    is an object $T$ of $\mathcal{C}$ with $\level(T)=2$.

    An enumeration of types $T$ of $\mathcal{C}$ is called a \textbf{collection of types} of $\mathcal{C}$
    if, and only if, for all objects $A$ and morphisms $f, g \colon A \to \father(T)$
    \[
        f^*T = g^*T \quad \Longrightarrow \quad f = g
    \]
\end{definition}
it is the object $\father(T)$ that we are interested in.

\begin{definition}
    a \textbf{doctrine of constructions} is a contextual category with products of families of types
    together with a collection of types $T$ such that for all $A, B \in \Ob \mathcal{C}$ with 
    $A \lhd B$ and $f \colon B \to \Prop$, where $\Prop = \father T$, 
    there exists a necessarily unique morphism $g \colon A \to \Prop$ also denoted by $\forall(f)$
    such that
    \[
        g^*T = \forall(f)^*T = \prod(f^*T)
    \]
\end{definition}

Now we will see that doctrines of constructions correspond to models of Calculus of Constructions.

\section{Interpretation of Calculus of Constructions}

In this section we will define the interpretation of Calculus of Constructions in arbitrary
doctrines of constructions. 
But first we need an auxiliary definition in order to be able to define  the interpretation in an inductive way.
\begin{definition}
    by structural induction of expressions we define a function \textbf{$\depth$} mapping
    pre-well-formed expressions and pre-contexts to $\omega$:
    \[\depth[x] = 1\]
    \[\depth[\Prop] = 1\]
    \[\depth[\Proof(A)] = \depth[A] + 1\]
    \[\depth[(\prod x \colon A) B] = \depth[A] + \depth[B] + 1\]
    \[\depth[(\lambda x \colon A) t] = \depth[A] + \depth[t] + 1\]
    \[\depth[(\forall x \colon A) t] =  \depth[A] + \depth[t] + 1\]
    \[\depth[\App([x \colon A] B, t, s)] = \depth[A] + \depth[B] + \depth[t] + \depth[s] + 1\]
    \[
        \text{For } \Gamma \equiv x_1 : A_1, \dots, x_n : A_n, 
        \quad
        \depth[\Gamma] = \sum_{i=1}^{n}\depth[A_i]
    \]
\end{definition}
